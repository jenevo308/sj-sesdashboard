About SES Serpentine Jarrahdale

The Serpentine Jarrahdale State Emergency Service (SES) is a volunteer-based emergency service unit that supports the local community during natural disasters and emergencies.

Volunteers respond to storms, floods, land searches, and other incidents, working closely with other emergency services to keep people safe.

Volunteer roles

Operational roles

  • Storm damage response: Tarping roofs, clearing fallen trees, and making areas safe.
  • Search and rescue: Assisting with land searches for missing persons.
  • Flood response: Sandbagging and supporting affected residents.

Support roles

  • Logistics & admin: Managing equipment, records, and coordination.
  • Communications: Radio operations and information flow during incidents.
  • Community engagement: Helping with events, education, and recruitment.

Volunteer requirements

  • Age: Usually 18+ (check with the unit for youth or cadet options).
  • Location: Live in or near the Serpentine Jarrahdale area.
  • Availability: Willing to attend training and respond to call-outs when possible.
  • Teamwork: Able to work safely as part of a team and follow instructions.
  • Checks: Able to complete any required background or medical checks.

Training and skills

You don’t need previous emergency services experience—full training is provided.

  • Safety & PPE: How to work safely and use protective equipment.
  • Storm & rescue skills: Roof work, ropes, ladders, and basic rescue techniques.
  • First aid: Basic first aid and casualty care (where available).
  • Radio & communication: Using radios and logging information during incidents.
  • Incident management: Understanding how emergency responses are coordinated.
